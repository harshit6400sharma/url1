What is Term Insurance

Term Insurance is the biggest financial security for your family in your absence!

It is the simplest and cheapest insurance policy available in the insurance market that provides financial benefits to the family of the insured in case of untimely death of the policy holder. The benefits are transferred to a family person nominated (nominee) by the insured. While buying a Term Insurance, the buyer can choose the term (period) of the policy, as per the options made available by a particular insurance provider - based on his/her age and other important parameters. Today, there are a huge number of insurance plans floating in the market fulfilling different needs of insurance buyers. Term Plan is definitely the most basic yet the most important insurance for every individual with a family.

Features and Benefits of Term Insurance

A lot of us desire for a simple and uncluttered life. A life where things are straight forward with minimal intrusion. Thus, there are no reasons as to why the same should change when it comes to life insurance. We seek out for insurance to secure a future without any financial stress on our loved ones. A term insurance fits into this description perfectly.

It is a simple life insurance plan that provides you with life cover only that means the family gets the assured sum only in the case of death of the policyholder and not otherwise within the term of the policy. You should also know that term insurance is economical while providing much higher coverage than traditional life insurance plans. Here are some key features benefits of Term life insurance.

Features

Free Look period

Insurance companies provide their customers with a free look period to go through the insurance policy. In the event of the policy not impressing them, they can opt to terminate without having to pay any sorts of surrender charges. The period is set at 15 days for policies purchased manually. People who purchase policies online have a larger window of 30 days to go through the policy documents and make up their minds.

Entry Age

For term insurance plans, the entry age is set at a minimum of 18 years and a maximum of 65 years. If you are within 18 to 65 years, you can opt for more than one term plan to cover a wider range of age.

Grace Period

Just like any other insurance product, customers enjoy the benefits of grace periods. The duration varies depending on the mode of payment that you have selected. For monthly payments, the grace period is usually 15 days. Whereas the same is 30 days for annual payment mode.

Revival

Being an insurance product at heart, you do get some advantages. If you have not paid your insurance premiums for a duration of two years, you can still revive your policy.

Premium Amount

This factor of the plan depends on the age of the applicant as well as the sum assured they are looking for. The plan is usually very affordable when taken at a younger age.

Type of Plan

Term plans provide you the flexibility of choosing a single plan for yourself or a joint plan. As the name suggests, the joint plan allows coverage for more than one life. One can also choose between a term plan that gives lump sum payout in the case of an eventuality or gives lump sum plus deferred incomes to the nominees

Benefits

Death

In the unfortunate event of death of the insured within the term of the policy, the nominee(s) stands to receive the sum assured. The benefits are exclusive only for the nominees mentioned in the policy document. Some life insurance companies also allow for putting multiple nominees

Maturity

A normal term insurance plan would not provide any benefits on maturity of the policy. However, off late several insurance companies have tweaked the term plans to provide some maturity benefits. These plans on maturity would pay you back the entire premium amounts paid till the end of policy, provided the insured sees the term through. However, they come at additional costs. These are called TROP i.e. Term Return of Premium pl

Tax

Just like any other insurance product, the applicant stands to benefit from taxation. The premium amounts paid are exempt from any form of income tax under Section 80(C) and 10 (10D) as per the Income Tax Act of 1961.

Term Insurance Additional Benefits (Riders)

Riders -Helping you ride on your financial independence with ease! Before you make a final decision on buying a term insurance, you must know the additional benefits with online Term Insurance Policy.

Various kinds of additional benefits with term insurance, offered by insurance providers, are called TERM INSURANCE RIDERS! These riders make your insurance policy much stronger by adding just a little more to your annual premium amount.

Here is a quick run through all kinds of riders available with term insurance policy:

Waiver of Premium

In case of a physical disability caused to the policyholder due to an accident or critical illness, they may not be able to work and pay future premiums. With this rider attached to the policy, all future premiums are waived off, yet, the policy remains active and the sum assured remains intact.

Accidental Death Benefit

If policyholder dies in an accident, this rider brings an additional sum along with the basic sum assured under the policy. For example, if the purchased policy is for a sum assured of Rs. 90 lacs and there is an accidental death benefit rider attached of Rs. 10 lacs, the family will be paid 90 lacs in case of natural death, and 1 Cr. in case of accidental death.

Critical Illness

If policyholder is diagnosed of a critical illness (all major illnesses specified in the policy document), a lump-sum amount is given for the treatment. After that the policy might continue or terminate as per the policy document. Also, if the death occurs due to this critical illness, the family is paid the additional benefit mentioned under the rider.

Return of Premium

Your term insurance policy becomes a great investment with this rider. If the policy expires and the policyholder is still alive, then this rider entitles them to get all the paid premiums back, which usually does not happen with a basic term plan.

Permanent And Partial Disability

If the policyholder is partially or permanently disabled due to accident, this rider helps to avail a percentage of the sum assured for next 5 to10 years. This rider is important to generate an income source for self in case of mishaps.

Income Benefit

This is majorly for the income generation for the family after the death of policyholder. Family gets a supplementary income every year for next 5 to 10 years, which is over and above the sum assured under the policy.

Term insurance riders should be a part of your financial planning. They make life easy for you and your family.

Types of Term Insurance

Term Insurance comes in various forms to cater to various needs of the customers.

  • Pure Term Plan

    It is the basic type of plan which one can buy as an insurance policy. Policyholder needs to pay the premium for the entire term of the plan and in case the policyholder dies before the term is completed, family gets the sum assured.

  • Return of Premium Plan

    It is the term insurance for people who look for some returns on the money they invested in buying the insurance. If the policyholder is still alive at end of the term, he gets back all the premiums that he paid during the term, which is a substantial amount to take care of a lot of expenses.

  • Decreasing Term Insurance

    This term plan comes with low premium as the sum assured keeps decreasing with every passing year. This kind of plan is bought keeping in view any debts on head. As the years pass and the policyholder remains alive, the debts get paid off from his income, hence the need for sum assured keeps decreasing too. If the policyholder dies during the term, the insurance money can be used to pay off the debts.

  • Increasing Term Insurance

    It is the opposite of decreasing term. Here, the sum assured keeps increasing every year. This type of insurance is bought, keeping in view larger future expenses like marriages and higher studies. Premiums may or may not change during the term but you can definitely compare various plans on ThePolicymall to buy a good Increasing Term with lower premiums.

  • Convertible Plan

    This term plans offer you the option of converting your basic term plan into a whole life insurance plan or investing in an endowment policy, after spending a stipulated amount of time in the pure term plan. This makes a good investment for good returns.

The Factors That Affect Premium

ThePolicymall is your number one search engine where you can look for the best suited insurance policy for you.
However, the factors that affect the premium that you need to pay are here:

Age

The younger youâ€™re, the lower will be the premium. Young people are less likely to die, therefore considered to be less risky by the insurance providers. For example; a person at the age of 25 buying insurance policy will have to pay lower premium than the person buying at the age of 50

Gender

Women are charged lower premiums as compared to men. A lot of scientific study and research conclude that women, on an average, live 5 years more than men. Which means that the insurance company gets premiums for a longer period, hence, the lower amount.

The Policy

The kind of policy one is investing in affects the premium. For example; if one is buying a life insurance cover for a long term and will be paying the premiums for longer duration, hence, they will pay lower amount of premium. However, if one buys a short-term plan, it will result in an increase in the premium amount.

Medical History

Even if one does not suffer from any ailment, but carries a family history of genetic disorders, then also they need to pay higher premiums on their insurance. For example; a person whose father, mother or anybody in immediate blood relation died of a disease that can be passed on, they will be charged higher premium.

Smoking

Given their lifestyle and unhealthy habits, the smokers are considered to be extremely risky customers and sometimes have to pay twice the premium as compared to a non-smoker.

Drinking

Consumption of alcohol as a habit is considered to be a risky behaviour by insurance companies. If one is a heavy drinker, it can lead to a substantial increase in premium

Health Record

Any diseases in past, surgeries in past, potential ailment developing in present, and the overall health record will all be taken in account while calculating the premium. For example; policy buyer suffering from ailments like diabetes or hypertension will be charged higher premium.

Profession

Risky professions like mining, working in oil and gas industry, fisheries and other such dangerous professions, lead to higher premiums.

Lifestyle Choices

Lifestyle determines the premium amount too. For example if one is involved in adventure sports, driving fast cars, partaking in risky activities, they will have to pay higher premiums. For example, a car racer, or a bungee-jumper will pay higher premiums.

Obesity

Obesity (fat body) leads to chronic health disorders like diabetes, blood pressure, heart ailments, joint complications, and many more. This puts the policy buyer in a high-risk customer category and results in higher premiums.

More Details: Eligibility Criteria for Term Insurance

Entry Age

You can buy a term insurance plan after the age of 18 and till the age of 65. Most people when they are young, do not feel the importance of buying term insurance. This is a big mistake that they make, merely for the reason, the younger you are the lesser the premium you need to pay. When you buy a policy early in life, you can get a good coverage by paying less. The premium goes higher and thus, sometimes due to affordability issues, you end up compromising on the coverage.

Policy Term

The minimum time for a policy allowed by most insurance companies is 5 years and the maximum of up to 65 years depending on the insurer. You should take a policy with a longer policy term so that you enjoy coverage for a long time. There is no maturity benefit in a term plan, thus, you should take a policy at least till the time you retire so that the income does not stop for the family if something were to happen to you.

Maturity Age

Usually, the term plans offered by insurance companies have a maturity age of 75 years to whole life. You must compare the policies offered by various companies and see the one which suits you the most in terms of the maturity age. Take a look at your financial situation while assessing the right term. Usually holding a policy till you retire is ideal, but you can increase or decrease the tenure depending on your needs.

Annual Premium

Term insurance can be bought for as minimal as Rs.2000 annually. The premiums go higher depending on the sum assured you opt for. There are options to pay monthly, quarterly and half yearly also if spending a lump sum is difficult. Your age and amount of coverage will have major impact on premium rate.

Financial Underwriting

The underwriting team in the insurance company will check your eligibility to take a policy by checking your Annual Income proof, which can be the Form 16 for the last financial year or last 3 monthsâ€™ salary slips for salaried individuals and Saral or ITR4 Form for Tax Filing for businessmen.

By checking the annual income the Financial Underwriters secede upon the Sum Assured that can be offered and they also check your ability to be able to pay regular premiums.

For example, Arun earns Rs. 8,000 a month and wishes to buy a term insurance policy with a sum assured of Rs. 2 CR. Chances are high that the insurance company will reject the application. This is because it is way more than the standard multiplier of 10-12 times the annual income unless he also shows some other income sources, family wealth, etc.

Medical Underwriting

When you apply for a term insurance policy, the underwriting team will look at your medical history. You must submit all information carefully and should not try to hide any important information. For a person who has diabetes or any other lifestyle disease, it becomes a high risk policy for the company and they might reconsider the policy or give a counter offer.

Moral Hazard

The insurance company should not sense any moral hazard when they receive an application for term insurance. The underwriting team will check insurability and insurable interest. If they sense anything out of the ordinary, then they reject the application. This usually happens when the proposer of the policy is different from the insured.

For example, a husband with a term cover of Rs. 30 lakhs opts for coverage of Rs.1 crore for his housewife. This is not acceptable to the insurance company and they may not grant the cover on grounds of possible Moral Hazard.

Conclusion

You should carefully buy a term insurance plan, as the financial future of your family is dependent on it. It is essential to understand the eligibility criteria and how each of these can affect your decision and the policy. Do your research about the company and the product before buying it. A term insurance is one of the best products you can take to secure the future of your family.
